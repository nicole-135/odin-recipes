Best Lasagna Ever

Description

This lasagna is a family favorite and is asked for by my husband and his family. Enjoy the abundance of cheese (ricotta, mozzarella and provolone to be exact) as you cut a hearty slice to serve with a nice side salad or a piece of warm focaccia bread.

Enjoy the decadent mixture of sausage, ground beef, tomatoes and fresh herbs that make this sinful yet light American Italian fare.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb. ground beef
  • 1 lb. hot italian sausage
  • 4 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 can (28 oz) whole tomatoes
  • 2 cans (6 oz) tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley (divided)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil (divided)
  • 2.5 tsp salt (divided)
  • 3 cups fresh ricotta cheese
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 10 ounces dried lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb sliced mozzarella
  • 1 lb slice provolone

Directions

  1. In a large skillet or saucepan, combine ground beef, sausage, and garlic. Cook over medium-high heat until browned. Drain some fo the fat. Add tomatoes, tomato paste, 1/2 of the fresh parsley and basil and 1 teaspoon salt. After adding the tomatoes, the sauce mixture should simmer for 45 minutes while you are working on the other steps.
  2. While the sauce is simmering, bring a large pot of water to a boil. And pre-heat oven to 350- degrees
  3. In a medium bowl, mix cottage cheese, beaten eggs, grated Parmesan, and the rest of the fresh parsley and basil, and 1 more teaspoon salt. Stir together well. Set aside. Add 1/2 teaspoon salt to the boiling water, then cook the lasagna noodles until “al dente” (not overly cooked). Drain.
  4. To assemble: Arrange 4 cooked lasagna noodles in the bottom of a baking pan, overlapping if necessary. Spoon half the cottage cheese mixture over the noodles. Spread evenly. Cover cottage cheese with a layer of mozzarella cheese. Spoon a little less than half the meat/sauce mixture over the top. Repeat, ending with meat/sauce mixture. Sprinkle top generously with extra Parmesan.
  5. Either freeze, refrigerate for up to two days, or bake immediately: 30 to 40 minutes, or until cheese has melted and browned and bubbly.
